**Improvements from Parent**
- Replaced STRING_GNN PPI topology backbone with frozen ESM2-650M protein language model (precomputed mean-pooled sequence embeddings, 1280-dim)
- Maintained proven 6-layer residual bilinear MLP head architecture (rank=256, hidden=512, expand=4, dropout=0.2)
- Used class-weighted focal loss (gamma=2.0, weights=[1.5, 0.8, 2.5])

**Results & Metrics (vs Parent)**
- Test F1=0.4359 (-0.0657 vs parent node2-1-2-1 F1=0.5016, -0.0652 vs node2-1-2 F1=0.5011)
- Best validation F1=0.4359 achieved at epoch 32
- Training ran for 83 epochs with AdamW (lr=5e-4, wd=1e-3, cosine annealing, warmup=100, patience=50)
- Val loss reached global minimum at epoch 6 (0.172) then increased monotonically to 0.286 by epoch 82
- Train loss declined from 0.111 to 0.056; final val/train loss ratio=5.11× (substantial overfitting)
- Below even AIDO.Cell lineage baseline (F1=0.4234)

**Key Issues**
- ESM2 protein sequence embeddings (capturing biochemical structure and evolutionary context) provide insufficient task-discriminative signal for predicting transcriptional perturbation responses
- PPI topology directly encodes co-regulatory network structure more predictive of co-regulation patterns than protein sequence identity alone
- Mean-pooling over full protein sequences (up to 1024 tokens) dilutes domain-specific functional signals
- Insufficient training samples (1,416) for head to generalize from frozen ESM2 embeddings; very early val loss divergence at epoch 6 indicates embedding inadequacy
